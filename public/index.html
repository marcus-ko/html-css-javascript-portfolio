UX Design Case Study

Overview

I designed a photo sharing app called WhatsLocal that would help identify trending places of interest, foods or products by grouping similiar images into categories.

Problem Statement

The idea came about when I realized I had been spending a lot of time searching for either places to take my family, a new place to dine, or even trying to find the latest tech gear. Reading through blogs can provide good information, when you have time to spare. Many families and even single adults are too busy to read and sift through articles.

What was the goal or desired outcome?

I wanted a faster way to find where other people around the city were visiting, what they were eating or buying. It is human nature to do things that others are doing. So I came up with an app that could visually display trends to help in making your decisions a lot quicker.

Users and audience

This app would be really intended for anyone wanting to know what the latest trends were.
  • Parents
  • Elderly
  • Young Adults

User Interview Questions

  • How much time do you spend looking for something to do?
  • Do you always know what to eat for dinner? If not, what do you do to get ideas.
  • When someone has something of interest, what do you say to them?
  • Does swiping down continually in an app annoy you?
  • What do you think of Instagram?
  • Would you eat something if 50 people in your area bought it?
  • What would make you go somewhere?
